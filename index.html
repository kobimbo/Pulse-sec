Core Message

Cybercrime in e-commerce poses significant risks, potentially compromising sensitive data and undermining consumer trust. E-commerce professionals must act as frontline defenders of both customer safety and digital integrity.

The guide emphasises a proactive approach: detect early, respond swiftly, contain damage, and restore operations.

Ghana-Specific Context

This material is tailored for e-commerce professionals in Ghana, acknowledging the rapid growth of online businesses and the increasing need for strong cybersecurity measures in local markets.

As digital commerce expands across the country, protecting customer data has never been more critical.

Overall Purpose

To raise awareness so e-commerce professionals treat digital devices and customer data as critical assets. The guide combines practical advice, warnings, and protocols to minimise data loss while ensuring a secure online shopping environment.

Key Sections & Highlights

What every e-commerce professional in Ghana needs to know

Parallels Between E-Commerce & Cybersecurity

Both domains share fundamental principles — Preparation, Detection, Response, and Recovery. E-commerce professionals' experience managing customer data directly relates to protecting digital evidence.

Modern Cybercrime Threats

  • Phishing attacks targeting customer credentials
  • Data breaches exposing payment information
  • Ransomware affecting online operations
  • Account takeovers leading to fraudulent transactions

Digital Evidence in E-Commerce Incidents

Professionals should identify and protect: customer databases, transaction logs, email communications, payment gateways, and web server data. These contain crucial information for determining the nature and source of cyber incidents.

Critical Do's & Don'ts

Avoid

  • Altering logs or deleting files
  • Using unsecured networks
  • Allowing unauthorised access

Do

  • Document incidents thoroughly
  • Secure affected systems
  • Notify cybersecurity experts immediately
  • Preserve all relevant data

Special Focus Areas

Payment Systems: Implement encryption and secure payment gateways to protect sensitive data.

User Authentication: Use multi-factor authentication to enhance account security and prevent unauthorised access.

Collaboration

E-commerce teams handle customer service and daily operations, while cybersecurity experts focus on incident response and data recovery. Joint training and clear communication channels are vital to an effective defence.

Safety Tips

  • Regularly update security protocols
  • Monitor for suspicious activity at all times
  • Educate all staff on recognising phishing
  • Document all security measures and incidents
